# DTC Financial Clarity System

A 3-Statement Stress Test Engine for Nigerian DTC Brands Operating at Scale

**Author:** Tobou Egbekun  
**Role:** Analytics Engineer & Financial Modeler  
**Location:** Lagos, Nigeria  

---

## Overview

A Nigerian DTC brand generating ₦40M/month can look profitable on paper — yet become insolvent within 60 days under operational stress.

This project models that reality.

The DTC Financial Clarity System is a fully integrated 3-statement financial model designed to answer one question:

> How long does the business survive when key variables break at the same time?

---

## The Problem

Most DTC operators manage by:

- Bank balance  
- Monthly revenue  

They do not model:

- RTO (Return-to-Origin rates)  
- Advertising efficiency  
- Logistics cost structure  
- Cash timing  

This creates a blind spot where businesses fail **suddenly, not gradually**.

---

## Key Insight

> RTO is not an operational metric. It is a financial survival metric.

At scale, failed deliveries compound into:

- Lost revenue  
- Double logistics cost  
- Accelerated cash burn  

---

## Model Structure

- **3-Statement Model**
  - Income Statement  
  - Cash Flow Statement  
  - Balance Sheet  

- **Scenario Engine**
  - Best Case  
  - Base Case  
  - Worst Case  

- **Key Features**
  - Cash runway tracking  
  - Scenario toggle (dynamic)  
  - Survival status indicator  

---

## Scenario Results

| Scenario   | Outcome |
|------------|--------|
| Best Case  | ₦150M+ cash, stable growth |
| Base Case  | ₦66M cash, viable |
| Worst Case | Insolvent within 60 days |

---

## Key Findings

1. The business is profitable under normal conditions but fragile  
2. Cash—not profit—is the true constraint  
3. RTO is the primary survival lever  
4. Fixed costs accelerate failure under stress  

---

## Strategic Insight

A ₦40M/month DTC brand is not a margin game.

It is a **cash survival system**.
